A much more abstract approach to lexical accentual specification is offered in the paper by Ben Hermans. He considers another classic case of tonal accent — the prosodic system of Limburgian varieties  — and argues that the difference between ‘Accent 1’ and ‘Accent 2’ is best analysed as one of underlying specified metrical structure (in particular feet). Hermans argues that his proposal is consistent with both the prosodic facts themselves and certain segmental developments dependent on tonal accent specifications, which are difficult to explain under a tonal accent.
