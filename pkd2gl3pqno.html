Anna Daugavet investigates Latvian and Lithuanian tonal accent from a synchronic perspective, evaluating how various tonal and segmental phenomena connected to tonal accent could be analysed with a tonal and a metrical approach. Rachel Fournier and Carlos Gussenhoven consider a case of contextual neutralization of an accentual contrast in Franconian tone accent dialect, which sheds light on pathways leading to the disappearance of tonal accent.   Ben Hermans proposes a metrical analysis of Limburgian tonal accent, where he argues that the local tone-accent contrast is derived from two types of feet, an even versus an uneven trochee.
